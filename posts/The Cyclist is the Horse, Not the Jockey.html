The Cyclist is the Horse, Not the Jockey


I've been thinking for a while about how to put into words the way it feels to me to push hard on a road bike, and today it hit me.

The cyclist is the horse, not the jockey.

When I walk into my apartment, my bike calls out from its hook on the wall hey you, take me outside, now. I respond the only way I can; okay, whatever you say.

While riding, I don't decide how fast to go. The bike says faster! I groan. I go faster. The bike says faster! again; forever.

I watch our shadows on the pavement as I pump away. The bike's is steady; calm. Mine is undulating about vigorously. The bike says good job, you majestic beast. I see in my shadow what the bike sees in me, and the praise makes me slide forward to the nose of the saddle and push even harder. I am power. Nothing more. Nothing less. Sweat pours off of me.

The bike reminds me there is no need to think. Just keep your head down, I will guide us. I oblige, focusing only on the cadence of my breathing. The bike whirs in response. Good. I never remember steering. The bike takes care of that.

On and on we go, locked in a euphoric dance.
