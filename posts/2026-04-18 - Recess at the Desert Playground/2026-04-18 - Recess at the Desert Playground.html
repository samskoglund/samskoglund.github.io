Experience Report

18 APR 2026

Recess at the Desert Playground


The Approach:

The trip began with my first plane ride since I was a wee lad. I managed to get what must be the best seat on an airbus A321, seat 27F. Check out this leg room! Ok maybe it's not better than the thrones that the bougie folks get (looking at you Hunter) but I thought it was pretty great.


Planes are cool! I was that guy that spent the whole flight reading the info, watching the tracker, and looking out the window. As an added bonus I was on emergency exit duty which was interesting to learn about. I was a bit disappointed to see that no one else seemed to be curious about any of this. But I suppose if you've been on a plane a bunch of times it's probably no more exciting than a car?

The actual approcahes were fun too. Lots of easy trail followed by some scrambling once closer to the routes. Cool plants everywhere. We moved fast.


Smooth Operator:

The first day we climbed this route called Smooth Operator (aka Sweet Crude). 6 pitches of trad, ranging from 5.6 to 5.9. It was a blast.


I wish pictures could convey the atmosphere better. The ones we took make it look so casual and small, but in real life it felt pretty legit, at least to me who doesn't have much multipitch experience yet.


There was one traverse on the 5.9 pitch that was an escpecially cool moment; I had a big smile on my face as I moved through it.


Dark Shadows:

Up next was Dark Shadows, 4 pitches of trad, ranging from 5.5 to 5.8. Again, our pictures don't do it justice. Check out some of the photos on Mountain Project if you want to see what this thing was really like. I was a total chicken on this one due to it feeling way more exposed than anything I've done before, so I made Hunter lead the whole thing.



Pitch 4 was the coolest. It involved moving away from the corner through a diagonal crack that you had to sort of lieback. It felt very commiting when there was 350 ft of air below. And I wasn't even leading! When Hunter did this sequence he let out a little yelp of what I assume was a blend of excitement and fright. In rock climbing that feeling is called FUN!

On the descent we encountered quite the statistical anomoly. Threw the rope down for the first rap and one of the ends skewered the next anchor 100 ft down. What are the odds!?


Reservations Required:

After a short rest in camp, we headed out to a new area and got on Reservations Required. 3 pitches of sport, ranging from 5.8 to 5.10a. The first pitch was awesome but way too short. The rest was flowy and enjoyable but nothing special.



The topout area would have been an awesome spot to bask in the sun, except there were 20mph winds raging over it while we were there.


Finished up the day with some single pitch.


First Pullout:

On the third day we found a low spot to sport climb in order to avoid the high winds. We climbed Sends in Low Places (5.8), Don't Stop Belaying (5.9), Routebusters! (5.10b), High-Hueco to Hell (5.10d), Un-Break My Hold (5.10d), and Hanging Fifty (5.11b).

As it got later in the day, we notcied rain drops falling despite the sky above being bright blue. It made no sense at first but once we were back driving the loop we could see that they were blowing over from the other side of the valley. The weather is wild in Red Rocks! Warm and sunny one moment and rainy with a sprinkle of hail the next. It was cool to watch how fast the clouds moved.


Blue Diamond Cave:

Unfortunately because of the rain the evening before our last day, we had to bail on our plan to hit up another multipitch. Sandstone soaks up and holds water for days, and it's brittle while wet. Luckily there was this little limestone place called Blue Diamond Cave nearby to play at.


I had never climbed on limestone before this, and was pleasantly surprised! All routes were full of insanely grippy slopey pinches and razor sharp edges. It was puzzling to find the good spots to hold amidst the chaotic texture, but once found they were solid. We climbed Commander's Quest (5.10b), Too Soon Old; Too Late Smart (5.10a), and Limerance for a Fool (5.10c).



Nature:

Lots of cool cacti everywhere. Hunter liked to refer to them as "danger bushes" and "danger barrels".




Reflections:

It was a pretty epic trip! Big thanks to Hunter for being an awesome adventure partner.
